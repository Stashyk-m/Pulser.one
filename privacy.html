Pulser ("we," "us," "our") operates the Pulser One mobile application and the website one.pulser.fit. We are committed to protecting your personal data in accordance with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR, and applicable national data protection laws.

Camera Data and On-Device Processing

The Pulser One camera feature processes your live video stream entirely on your device using the MediaPipe Pose Landmarker model. No video footage, raw frames, or image data is ever transmitted to our servers or any third party. The only data that leaves your device is the computed output — anonymised joint angle scores and rep counts — which may be stored in your account to power progress tracking.

Health Connect and HealthKit

With your explicit consent, we access health metrics from Android Health Connect or Apple HealthKit to compute your injury risk score and personalise your training programme. This data:

  • Is read only — we do not write to Health Connect without your explicit action
  • Is not sold to any third party
  • Is not used for advertising purposes
  • Can be disconnected at any time in the app Settings
  • Is stored encrypted in your account and deleted when you delete your account

Body Scan Data

Body scan results — including estimated body shape, frame size, proportions, and composition estimates — are stored locally on your device and optionally in your encrypted account for progress comparison. These results are derived from 2D keypoint measurements and are clearly labelled as estimates. We do not share this data externally.

Waitlist and Email Communications

When you join the waitlist at one.pulser.fit, we store your email address and any optional information you provide (name, device platform, fitness goal) to send you updates about Pulser One's launch. We use formsubmit.co to process waitlist submissions. Emails are sent via standard email infrastructure. You can unsubscribe from any email by clicking the unsubscribe link or emailing go@pulser.fit.

Cookies and Tracking

The one.pulser.fit website uses:

  • ipapi.co — a single anonymous API call to detect your country for regional pricing and language auto-detection. No persistent identifier is stored.
  • localStorage — stores your language preference locally in your browser. No data is transmitted.

We do not use advertising cookies, cross-site tracking, or analytics platforms that identify individuals.

Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as you have an active account or as necessary to provide the Service. Exercise history and body scan results are retained for the duration of your account. Waitlist email addresses are retained until you unsubscribe or the Service launches. You may request deletion at any time.

Your Rights (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, UK, or Switzerland, you have the following rights:

  • Access — request a copy of your personal data
  • Rectification — correct inaccurate personal data
  • Erasure — request deletion of your personal data
  • Portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Restriction — request we limit processing of your data
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Withdraw consent — at any time, without affecting prior processing

To exercise any right, contact us at go@pulser.fit. We will respond within 30 days.

Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 16. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such data, contact us immediately at go@pulser.fit.

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, or destruction. Health and body scan data is encrypted at rest and in transit. However, no transmission over the internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

International Transfers

If you are located outside Poland, your data may be transferred to and processed in countries that may have different data protection laws. Where required, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) before transferring your data.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of material changes by email or via an in-app notification. The updated Policy will be posted at one.pulser.fit/privacy with a revised effective date.

Contact and Complaints

For privacy questions or requests, contact: go@pulser.fit

If you are dissatisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. In Poland, this is the Urząd Ochrony Danych Osobowych (UODO) at uodo.gov.pl. If you are located in another EU member state, you may contact your national supervisory authority.
